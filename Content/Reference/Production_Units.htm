Production Units Overview

Defining the Production Units and Conversions involves multiple Configuration screens. The unit conversion infrastructure is based on the following fundamentals:

  1. Products (Gas and NonGas) can be represented in both Volumetric and Quality terms.
  2. The fundamental Volumetric unit is m3.
  3. The fundamental Quality units are:
    1. For Gas - J (joule).
    2. For NonGas - kg (kilograms)
  4. A specific Unit will be either a Gas, NonGas or Equivalence unit. For Gas or NonGas units, the unit is either Volumetric or Quality. For example:
    • mcf is a Gas Unit that is associated with / converted to m3.
    • btu is a Gas Unit that is associated with / converted to Joules.
    • bbl is a NonGas unit that is associated with / converted to m3.
    • T (Metric Tonne) is a NonGas unit that is associated with / converted to kg.
  5. An Equivalence unit allows conversion between Product Categories.
    • These are expressed as conversion factors to boe.
  6. Product Quality Units and conversions are defined in terms of the Fundamental Units:
    1. For Gas - conversion to J/m3.
    2. For NonGas - conversion to kg/m3.
  7. Products are defined in terms of:
    • Their default Equivalence conversion (from an appropriate Volume unit to boe)
    • Their default Quality conversion (to an appropriate Product Quality unit)

 

The Configuration sequence is:

Create the Production Units:

  • Abbreviation (what will be displayed in any drop down list)
  • Description - a text field that will be displayed in Report Headers.
  1. For a Gas product:
    • Volumetric conversion to m3
    • Energy conversion to J
  2. For a Non-Gas product (liquids, solids)
    • Volumetric conversion to m3
    • Mass conversion to kg
  3. For an Equivalence unit
    • Conversion factor to boe

Enter the desired Product Qualities Units

  1. For Gas products:
    • Description - a text field that will be displayed in appropriate drop down lists
    • Conversion factor to J/m3
  2. For NonGas products:
    • Description - a text field that will be displayed in appropriate drop down lists
    • Conversion factor to kg/m3

Create the Products. For each Product:

    • Bookable - whether the production will be included in any metric calculations
    • Category - the Category the Product belongs to (typically Gas, Liquids or Other)
    • Energy - identifies that the Product's Quality will be based on Energy units
    • Units/boe - the conversion factor for the product to the base equivalence unit (boe)
    • Unit - the unit that corresponds to the Units/boe conversion factor
    • Quality - the default Quality for the product
    • Quality Unit - the Product Quality Unit (defined above) that corresponds to the Quality conversion.

Define the required production Rate units

    • Abbreviation - what will appear in drop down lists
    • Description - for information purposes
    • Volume - a selection of existing Production units
    • Time - the corresponding period for the unit - Daily and Monthly are most common

Note that a Monthly rate is converted to an equivalent Daily Rate for production calculations.

 

Production Units

 

Note: a Unit can only have one conversion entered, it is either a Volumetric unit (converts to m3), Quality (converts to J or kg), or Equivalence (converts to boe).

The column that the conversion is entered in defines what type of Unit it is (Volumetric or Quality), and how this unit will be utilized within the conversion infrastructure. The m3 (energy) and m3 (mass) headers indicate which column to enter the Volumetric conversion factor into: a Gas unit is entered under m3 (energy) and Non-Gas unit under m3 (mass).

With the exception of Equivalence units, the conversion factors entered on this page should be standard conversions. When providing Volumetric conversions, they should all use the same Reference state, i.e.; mcf and mmcf should be factors of 1000 and 1000000 to the cf conversion.

Equivalence units will be based on your company's practice, however, similar units (mcfe and mmcfe or mboe and mmboe) should be numerically consistent.

 

Product Quality Units

esi.manage uses J/m3 (Gas) and kg/m3 (Non-Gas) as the internal Quality Units. Your company may require the ability to report in other Quality Units, such as btu, GJ, kg or lb.  

The Product Qualities table is where you will provide the conversion between your companies desired units, and the internal units. When creating a Product, you will need to specify the default Quality in terms of either an internal unit, or a unit defined here.

 

Products

Individual Products are defined in terms of:

  1. Bookable - whether the Product is to participate in metric (Lifting Cost, F&D) calculations.
  2. Energy - indicates whether the Quality will be represented in Energy or Mass units.
  3. Units/boe, Unit  - the Base unit for the particular Product and its conversion to the base Equivalence Unit (boe).
  4. Quality, Unit - the conversion of the Product to an existing Quality Unit.

 

 

Production Rate Units

Production Rate Units are used as part of Forecasting a Production segment, the Abbreviations will appear in the drop down list to specify the Rate that corresponds to the IP.

The Description is the label that will appear when reporting Production as a Daily Rate.

 

 

Note: Monthly rate units will be interpreted based on the Average Number of Days in a month.

 

Reporting Production

A given Production stream can be reported in two ways, depending on the Product Category:

  • Gas - Volumetric or Energy
  • Non-Gas (Liquid or Other) - Volumetric or Mass

In addition, there are Equivalence Units which allow for cross category reporting - for example Reporting the entire production in terms of a Gas (mcfe) or Liquid (boe). These units will only have a "notional" representation of Quality - for example a GJe unit would be based on a "standard" Heat Content for both Gas and Non-Gas.
