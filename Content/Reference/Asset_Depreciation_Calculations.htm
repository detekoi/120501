Depreciation Calculations

Capital Depreciation is calculated on an Expenditure by Expenditure basis, and is used to calculate the After Tax amount for an Analysis Case.

  1. Depreciation Models can be configured for each Expenditure Type, on a Jurisdiction by Jurisdiction basis.
  2. When the Depreciation Models have been configured, they will be calculated automatically for every expenditure during the Analysis Case run.
  3. The Default models can be overridden at the Expenditure line within a Project.

 

For each Expenditure/Jurisdiction pair, the values that can be entered are (note that there are equivalent, but independent options for the Tangible and Intangible portions of the Expenditure Type):

  • Type - choices of Depreciation models (Declining Balance, Straight Line, Sum of Years, Unit of Production). These models determine the amount of the original capital cost that is eligible for deduction from the Net Income at the point in time as defined by the Convention.
  • Annual Decline % - an Annual Depreciation rate (if Declining Balance is selected as the Depreciation model.
  • Useful Life (Years) - the duration the Depreciation is applied over (the Asset is assumed to be out of service at the end of this period.
  • Convention - defines when the Depreciation calculations are performed - Mid Month, Mid Quarter or Mid Year.

The values that are defined for Depreciation in the Configuration are applied by default to any Expenditure (based on the Jurisdiction assigned to the Project). These settings can be overridden at the Project level.

 

Useful Life

The Useful Life establishes how many Years the Expenditure will be depreciated over. In terms of the Default Models, the Salvage value is presumed to be 0, meaning the entire amount (Tangible or Intangible) will be depreciated.

There is the Option to supply a Salvage Value against each Expenditure, in which case:

Amount to be Depreciated = Expenditure Amount - Salvage Value

 

Convention

Depreciation is always calculated internally as a Yearly Amount, which is then allocated evenly over the Periods in the year. The Convention parameter works in conjunction with the Expenditure End Date to determine what portion of Expenditure will be depreciated in the first (and last) years.

  • Mid-Year - this is the most typical convention; regardless of where the Expenditure End Date falls, 1/2 of a full year's depreciation is applied in the first, and last years.
  • None - this treats the Expenditure as having a full year's depreciation, regardless of when the Expenditure Occurs.
  • Mid-Month - 0.5 months for the Month the Expenditure ends in, plus the remaining months of the year.
  • Mid-Quarter - 0.5 quarters for the Quarter the Expenditure ends in, plus the remaining quarters of the year.

The Convention doesn't simply adjust the Percentage for the first year. There is a Decline Amount (based on the selected method) calculated for each year of the useful life. The Convention is the mechanism by which the Economic Periods are overlaid onto the Decline profile. For example, using a Mid Year convention:

  1. Economic Period 1 (the first year where Depreciation occurs) will have 1/2 of the first year's calculated Depreciation.
  2. Economic Period 2 will have 1/2 of the first year's calculated Depreciation, and 1/2 of the second year's.

 

Straight Line Method

The Straight Line Method takes the Useful Life and converts it to an Annual Decline Percentage (the Annual Decline Percentage cannot be entered in the Depreciation Table):

 Annual Decline Percentage = 1 / Useful Life

The Yearly Depreciation Amount is:

Yearly Depreciation Amount = Amount to be Depreciated * Annual Decline %

The yearly Depreciation Amount shown in the Cash Flow Analysis will be impacted based on the selected Convention.

 

Declining Balance Method

The Declining Balance Method provides for an accelerated Decline in the early year's of the Useful Life. A Straight Line Decline is calculated in parallel (based on the amount to be depreciated at the start of each year), and switches to the Straight Line Method when it's Depreciation amount exceeds what would be calculated from the Declining Balance Method (which will never fully depreciate an Expenditure).

The Declining Balance Method requires Decline Percentage to be entered - typically double the Decline Percentage for the Straight Line Method, i.e., assuming a Useful Life of 7 years:

Annual Decline % straight line = 1 / 7 = 14.29%

Annual Decline % declining balance = 1 / 7 * 2 = 28.57%

The Annual Decline % is not automatically calculated from the Useful Life, you must enter the appropriate value.

The calculation sequence is:

  1. Calculate the Depreciation Amount of the Remaining Balance:
    • Depreciation Amount current year = Remaining Balance current year * Annual Decline % declining balance method
  2. In parallel, calculate the Deprecation Amount using the Straight Line Method:
    1. The Straight Line Annual Decline % is re-calculated based on the number of remaining years - for example, in year 5 of a 7 Year useful life, 3 would be used to calculate the Decline %.
    2.  Depreciation Amount current year = Remaining Balance current year * Annual Decline % straight line method
  3. Whichever Depreciation Amount is greater will be used for the current year's depreciation.
    • The mathematics are such that once the 'switch' to the Straight Line method occurs, it will always be selected for all remaining periods.
  4. Calculate the Remaining Balance
    • Remaining Balance next year = Remaining Balance this year - Depreciation Amount

The yearly Depreciation Amount shown in the Cash Flow Analysis will be impacted based on the selected Convention.

 

Sum of Years Method

The Sum of Years is also an Accelerated Decline method. The Annual Decline % will be calculated based on the Useful Life, the Annual Decline % cannot be entered with this method.

  1. Calculate the Sum of Years based on the Useful Life:
    1. Useful Life = 7, Sum of Years = 7 + 6 + 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 28
    2. Useful Life = 5, Sum of Years =  5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 15
  2. For a particular year in the Depreciation Schedule:
    • Depreciation Amount = Amount to be Depreciated x (Years Remaining / Sum of Years)
  3. For example, in the 3rd year of a 7 year useful life:
    • Depreciation Amount = Amount to be Depreciated x (5 / 28)

The yearly Depreciation Amount shown in the Cash Flow Analysis will be impacted based on the selected Convention.

 

Unit of Production Method

The Unit of Production method for depreciation expresses the useful life in terms of the total number of units expected to be produced for a given Pool of Projects.

  1. Calculate the Pools’ UoP Depreciation Rate: Un-Depreciated CAPEX/ Remaining Reserves
  2. For each period, take the UoP Rate and multiply by the production in that period.
  3. The UoP Rate is calculated every month to account for additional CAPEX and Reserves being added to the pool.

The yearly Depreciation Amount shown in the Cash Flow Analysis will be impacted based on the selected Convention.
