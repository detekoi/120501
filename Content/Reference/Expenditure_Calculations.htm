Expenditure Calculations

An Expenditure is Forecast using 3 fields:

  1. Start Date - when the activity is forecasted to begin.
  2. End Date - when the activity is forecasted to complete
  3. Gross Amount - the Total amount of the Expenditure

The Start to End Dates are termed the Forecast Period. In terms of the 'duration':

  • the Start Date is interpreted as occurring at 12:00:00 AM.
  • the End Date is interpreted as occurring at 11:59:59 PM
  • As a result, an Expenditure with the same start and end dates has a duration of 1 day.

 

Expenditure Schedule/Details

Associated with each Expenditure is a 'Forecast Schedule'. This is the middle table on the Expenditure page. The schedule entries will be initialized based on the duration of the Expenditure. For example:

  1. An Expenditure with a 1 year duration will be distributed equally over 12 monthly Schedule Entries (first of month, last of month)
  2. An Expenditure that covers multiple years will be distributed equally over yearly entries.
  3. An Expenditure with a 1 month duration will have a single entry for the entire amount.
  4. An Expenditure with less than 1 month duration will be distributed into weekly (7 day) entries, based on an average daily rate (Gross Amount/duration. If there is a resultant schedule period less than 7 days, the Gross Amount for that period will be the number of days x the daily rate.
  5. If the Forecast Period overlaps a month end, there will be Schedule entry for each month. The number of days within each month will determine the allocation of the Gross Amount.
  6. If the Forecast Period covers multiple months, entries will be created for each month, with the Gross Amount being distributed based on the number of days within any period.

The Expenditure Details are Editable, and there is a feature to Expand entries into smaller duration entries, i.e., a yearly Schedule Entry can be expanded into Quarterly or Monthly entries. This is an unidirectional change. If the expected capital spend is non-uniform over the period, the schedule can be adjusted to better represent the capital profile, as well as facilitate tracking.

Expenditure Calculations

All Expenditure (CAPEX) Forecast calculations are based on the corresponding Schedule entry(s) for the period in question. How the schedule information is used is dependent on 3 factors:

  1. The Date Range for the calculations.
  2. The periodicity of the calculations (monthly, quarterly, annual).
  3. The Last Actuals Date (if Blending is being used).

The combination of Date Range and Periodicity will not always align with the Schedule of the Expenditures. For example:

  1. An Expenditure has 4 Schedule Entries: Jan 3 to Jan 9, Jan 10 to Jan 16, Jan 17 to Jan 23 and Jan 24 to Jan 26.
  2. A weekly Report (12 period) is being generated, starting on Jan 1, which results in the following periods starting: Jan 1, Jan 8, Jan 15, Jan 22, Jan 29, Feb 4, Feb 11, etc.
  3. Each of the Schedule Entries has an average daily rate (Gross Amount for the period / duration).
  4. This daily rate is used in conjunction with the number of days that it has within a given reporting period.

e.g.: the first Schedule period (Jan 3 to Jan 9) has 5 days within reporting period 1, and 2 days within reporting period 2.

The second reporting period (beginning on Jan 8) is made of 2 days of Schedule Entry 1, and 5 days of Schedule Entry 2.

 

Blended Calculations

For Blended calculations, there is an additional degree of division:

  • The Last Actuals Date separates the data calculations into two portions.
    1. Calculation Start to Last Actuals Date (interpreted as an End Date)
    2. Last Actuals Date + 1 to Calculation End.
  • Within Period 1, only Expenditure Data of Type Actuals is considered.
  • Within Period 2, Forecast Data is used.

Capital Actual entries are represented in the same manner as the Capital Forecast Schedule - Start and End Date, and Amount. The principles described above about calculating a Daily Rate (based on an Entry Period) and using this value in conjunction with a particular Calculation Period applied to both Actuals and Forecast entries. The Last Actuals Date simply introduces an additional transition date.

 

Expenditure Modifiers

There are two Modifiers that are used in Expenditure Calculations:

  • WI% - used for Net Basis calculations.
  • Risk - used for Risked calculations

See Risk

Risk is described by the AF% and OP% values in the Dependencies tab. Note that by definition Actuals or Field Estimates have 100% probability.

Working Interest is inherited from Ownership for each Expenditure Schedule entry, Forecast, Field Estimates and Actuals. As indicated above, Expenditure Calculations are based on the data contained within the Schedule table(s). If a Net calculation is being requested, it will be based on the Net Amounts for the individual schedule entries, within the context of the Dates logic described above.
