Economic Calculations

The Analysis Case will perform Discounted Cash Flow calculations against a collection of Projects. There are several aspects to the calculations:

  1. The underlying cash flow is generated at a monthly granularity. The Display settings determine whether this data will be presented in a monthly periodicity, or aggregated into yearly values.
    • The Cash Flow data is generated based on the CAPEX and Production Types and Definitions specified in the Analysis Case Properties for the Horizon set in the Schedule.
    • The underlying Data is generated per Expenditure, OPEX and Production Segment. The Results can be displayed for each individual entry.
    • Revenue will be calculated by combining the calculated Production with the selected Price File.
    • If a Currency Escalation file has been selected, the costs are adjusted accordingly.
  2. The Cash Flow, Burdens, Depreciation and Taxes are calculated against every Asset.
    • Burdens and Taxes are either based on the corresponding Jurisdiction, or what has been locally overridden in the Project.
    • The Depreciation is calculated based on the configured Expenditure Depreciation model, or what has been locally overridden within the Project.
    • These results are also aggregated  
  3. Depending on the structure of the Project(s) / Project Set(s) being analyzed, the Asset level data described above are then aggregated to the various levels of the Cash Flow Analysis hierarchy:
    • Asset to Project
    • Project to Jurisdiction
    • Jurisdiction to Project Set
    • Project Set to Analysis Case
  4. The Discounting is then applied to the Asset Level data, and at each aggregate level.

Terminology

These terms will be used in the following description:

  • Income Tax – a % that is applied to determine the Tax owed. This is referred to in the following description as Local Tax or Government Tax.
  • Production or Revenue Tax – defined against individual Product’s Production or Revenue.
    • These are referred to in the following description as Production/Revenue Tax.
  • Production taxes are expressed as $ / volume and are independent of the Price File being used to calculate Cash Flow.
  • Revenue taxes are expressed as a % and are dependent on the Price File being used to calculate Cash Flow.

 

The Analysis Case uses the term "Cash Flow Tag" to represent calculation points in the Cash Flow:

  • Net Revenue (NRev) – Revenue less Operating Expenses
  • Net Net Revenue (NNRev) – Net Revenue less Local Production/RevenueTax
  • Before Tax (BTax) – Net Net Revenue less CAPEX
  • After Tax (ATax) – Net Net Revenue less (Local Tax, Government Production/Revenue Tax, Depreciation, Government Tax and CAPEX)

 

Discounting

The cash flow table that is generated is based on a monthly periodicity. In terms of the discounting calculations, these amounts are treated as if they occur at the end of the month.

The Annual Rate will be used for any Period whose Discounting is Annual. There is the option to select Monthly Discounting for some period of the Analysis Horizon.

If Monthly Discounting is being used for a particular period, the Effective Monthly Discount Rate is calculated as:

Effective Monthly Discount Rate = (1 + Annual Rate)^(1/12) - 1

For example, a 15% Annual Discount Rate converts to an Effective Monthly Rate of ~1.1715%

The Discounting that is applied to a particular period is based on the Discount Rate (Effective Monthly or Annual) and the number of Discount Periods. Again, the Number of Discount Periods is dependent on the Display / Discounting settings.

For Annual Discounting:

Number of Discount Periods = (Year Current Period - Year Economic Discounting As Of Date) + (12 - MonthEconomic Discounting As Of Date )/12

eg.: with Discount As Of Date of Dec 31, 2009, any period in 2013 will have a value of 4 for the number of Discount Periods. Even if the Display Setting is Monthly, if Annual Discounting has been selected for that particular period, the number of Discount Periods will be 4 for all monthly entries within 2013.

If you have selected an Economic Discount Date somewhere other than the end of a year, the formula above accounts for the 'mid-year' transition.

When Monthly Discounting has been selected:

Number of Discount Periods = (Serial Month Current Period - Serial Month Economic Discounting As Of Date)

effectively the number of Months between the Current Period and the Economic Discount Date.

The Mid Year option is only applicable to Periods that have Annual Discounting (the Mid-Period correction will never be applied to a period with Monthly Discounting).

Number of Discount Periods Mid Year  = Number of Discount PeriodsAnnual Discounting - 0.5

The calculation of a Discounted Value is:

Discounted Amount = Amount * ( 1 / (1 + Discount Rate) ^ (Number of Discount Periods) )

 

Economic Metrics

Each Metric is associated with a specific Cash Flow Tag (one of the list above), which specifies the ‘point’ in the Cash Flow calculations that is used in the Metric. In the following descriptions of the Metric, the term Cash Flow Tag will mean any of the above list.

  • Horizon – the period which is covered by the Start Date (either Point Forward Date or Full Cycle First Spend) plus the Span
  • Cumulative CAPEX (∑ CAPEX) – the total CAPEX which is captured over the Horizon
  • Cumulative Net Revenue / Before Tax / Net Net Revenue / After Tax (∑NRev, ∑NNRev, ∑Btax, ∑BTax) – corresponding cumulative amounts of these Tags

Metrics can be configured at a different Discount Rate than what is being used for the Analysis Case itself. The other properties of the Analysis Case (Discounting Schedule, Economics As Of Date, etc.) are used to generate the corresponding Discounted Cash Flow (if it is a Discounted Metric) for the Metric.

 

IRR – Internal Rate of Return

The Internal rate of return is the Discount Rate which will result in NPV = 0.

 

Economic Limit

The Economic Limit is defined as the point in time when the periodic Cash Flow goes from Positive to Negative.

 

Payout

The Payout is the date when the Cumulative Cash Flow (at the chosen Cash Flow Tag) equals the Cumulative CAPEX captured over the entire Horizon.

The Payout start date is defined as the later of the project start date and the point-forward date defined in an Analysis Case.

When creating a Payout metric there is a flag to Interpolate Date. This option allows for an interpolation of when the Payout occurred, and will calculate the Payout to a specific date. When a start date occurs on any day of the month other than the first and date interpolation is on, the month’s value will be reduced proportionately.

 

Custom Metric

Custom economic metrics allow the user to specify a formula as a metric calculation. The formula is a simple NUM / DENOM, where NUM and DENOM are sums of cashflow columns, where each column can be separately filtered

The usual metrics NPV, PIR, DPIR, ROI, DROI can be defined as a custom metric. As information, the metrics calculation are referenced below.

 

NPV - Net Present Value

Net Present Value at the specified Analysis Case Discount Rate is reported as the Cumulative Discounted Cash Flow at a particular level (i.e., Asset or Project).

The NPV Metric is defined as: ∑ Discounted "Cash Flow Tag"

Additional NPV metrics can be added which repeat the Cash Flow Discounting calculations at a different Discount Rate and report the cumulative value as described above as the NPV at that rate.

 

Profit to Investment Ratio (PIR)

The Profit to Investment Ratio (PIR) is defined as:

PIR = (∑”CashFlowTag” + ∑ CAPEX) / ∑ CAPEX

 

Discounted Profit to Investment Ratio (DPIR)

The Discounted Profit to Investment Ratio (DPIR) is defined as:

DPIR = (∑Discounted ”CashFlowTag” + ∑ Discounted CAPEX) / ∑ Discounted CAPEX

 

Return on Investment (ROI)

The Return on Investment (ROI) is defined as:

ROI = ∑”Cash Flow Tag” / ∑ CAPEX

 

Discounted Return on Investment  (DROI)

The Discounted Return on Investment (DROI) is defined as:

DROI = ∑ Discounted “Cash Flow Tag” / ∑ Discounted CAPEX
