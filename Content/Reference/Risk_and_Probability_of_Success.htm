Dependency, Risk and Probability of Success

Dependency is a mechanism to relate one Measure to a particular Expenditure. This is accomplished using the Dependencies Tab of a Project for Expenditure, Production, Resource, and OPEX entries.

Selecting a Dependent On expenditure will:

  • Enforce Date Rules
    • If End -> Start linkage is selected, the Start Date for the Measure will be No Earlier Than the End Date of the previous measure.
    • If Start -> Start linkage is selected, the Start Date for the Measure will be No Earlier Than the Start Date of the previous measure
  • Calculate NPD% and NPS% (Net Probability of Doing and Net Probability of Success)
    • NPD% and NPS% are calculated based on 4 factors
      1. AF% (Activity Factor) – this is the probability that the activity occurs and will cap the NPD% and NPS% for all subsequent measures in the chain.
      2. OP% (Outcome Probability) – this is the probability that once an activity is started, it will complete successfully.
      3. Failure – If this value is checked, the correlated expenditure will only occur when its parent node in the dependency chain fails. Thus, any AF% or OP% values entered for this activity will only effect calculations if the parent entity fails.
      4. Reset NPD% - If this value is checked, the NPD% for that dependency chain is reset to 100% at that measure level.

 

There are 3 primary reasons for utilizing Risk and/or Dependencies:

  1. Establishing a consistent Schedule within a Project. This prevents an Onstream Date being Forecast prior to the Completion.
  2. Project Comparison or Evaluation - it is a way of levelling two Projects - high risk and high production, versus lower risk and less production.
  3. Providing a more accurate Forecast of the company's (business unit, asset team) plan for the coming year, or position at the end of the year, since it is unlikely that all activities will be successful (production impact) and not all capital will be spent (Completion and Tie-In won't occur for a dry well).

Probability of Doing (PD%) and Net Probability of Doing (NPD%)

When using Measure chains to 'calculate' the Probability of Success for the various outcomes, it is necessary to enter the AF% and/or OP% for each of the expenditures in the chain.

  • Generally the AF%/OP% for a particular Expenditure will be the historical 'average' of similar activities in a particular area.
  • The AF% represents the probability of successfully starting the item in the chain while the OP% represents successfully completing the item in the chain. For example:
  1. The AF% of the Drill expense is 100% - it is planned to be done. Since it's the first expenditure in the chain, it's NPD% is 100%
  2. The OP% of the Drill expense might be 80% - its likelihood of occurring is the average success rate of drills in that area.
  3. The NPD% and NPS% of the Drill expense are 100% and 80% respectively.
  4. The OP% of the Completion expense might be 90%. This represents the historical success rate of the Casing activity in that area.
  5. The NPS% of the Completion expense is 72% -  NPS% Drill x OP% Completion.
  6. The OP% of the Tie-In expense might be 100%. If the Completion activity begins, Tie-In will as well.
  7. The NPS% of the Tie-In expense is also 72% -  NPS% Completion x OP% Tie-In.

 

Whenever a Risked Expenditure value is being calculated, the Gross Amount for the period is multiplied by the current NPS% for the Expenditure.

 

Branching Chains - the Failure Case

Generally there are Abandonment costs associated with an unsuccessful Drilling activity. This particular Expenditure is identified by the Negative flag, and will be made Dependent on the most appropriate Expenditure in the Chain (for example the Drill or the Case).

The AF% of the failure Expenditure will be 100% - its likelihood of occurring is captured in the historical averages of the preceding expenditures. Since the 'failure' branch of the chain is applicable only when Risk is being used:

  • An Expenditure with a negative flag will only be 'calculated' when a Risked (Gross or Net Risked) is selected for the calculations.
