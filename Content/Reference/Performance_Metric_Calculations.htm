Performance Metric Calculations

Project Metrics can be calculated in four locations:

  1. The Key Metrics page of an individual Project.
  2. Reports such as Project Summary or Project Variance Summary
  3. As a Ranking Metric
  4. Economic Analysis as part of a Custom layout or Metrics tab

Depending on where the Metric is calculated, there are different choices available for the Horizon as well as Types and Definitions.

  • Key Metrics - will always be calculated in both a Net Risked, and Net Unrisked basis, over the Budget Period (Horizon). The Budget Units will define in what units a particular Metric will be calculated in.
  • Ranking - will be calculated using the specified Ranking Units. Values will always be calculated on a Net Basis, using Blended numbers. Risk is an optional modifier for any Metric.
  • Reports have full configuration capabilities for Types and Definitions, as well as the Units for the metric calculations.

 

Budget Units

Metrics are presented in “Budget Units” as set in the Budget Configuration. Note this is distinct from reporting units, which are always selectable.  

The product chosen serves as an equivalency, which production and reserves are converted to.  Appropriate units for Production, Reserves, F&D and Capital Efficiency are selected when creating a new budget.  

These “Budget Units” are used when calculating performance metrics and displayed as such in the Project Summary.  For example, when Canadian Dollars and barrels of oil are selected in the Budget Configuration, F&D is measured in Canadian dollars per barrel.

See Budget Units

 

Metric Time Periods

In the metric calculation three different time periods are available:

  1. TB (B stands for “Budget”) is the budget time period.  Note that a budget can be any arbitrary span of time, but is typically one year.  The budget start and end date are set in configuration when creating a new budget.
  2. TFS (FS stands for “First Spend”) is a one year time period starting from the earliest expenditure for a project.
  3. TOS (OS stands for “First Onstream Date”) is a one year period starting from the earliest product Onstream date for a project.
  4. Earliest Project Date
  5. First Reserves Booking
  6. First Rig Release
  7. First Spud
  8. WI stands for Working Interest
  9. NPD stands for Net Probability of Doing for Capital expenditures, Production, and OPEX.
  10. NPS stands for Net Probability of Success for Capital expenditures, Production, and OPEX.

These different time periods are used to sum capital expenditures, reserves, production and operating cost.  Depending on the metric, different time periods, and in some cases, multiple time periods are used in calculating the metric.  In the following definitions, the time period is explicitly stated.

 

Metric Definitions

Capital Expenditures

Capital Expenditures are the sum of a project’s expenditures over the budget interval.

  • Unrisked Gross - This is the sum of the working interest, gross expenditures within the Budget period.  The Unrisked Sales Capital Expenditures equation for n expenditure records is:

∑n (Gross Expenditures within TB)

Net Sales:  This is the sum of the working interest, risked expenditures within the budget period.  The Risked Sales Capital Expenditures equation for n expenditure line items is:

∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn )

where NPD is the Net Probability of Doing.

 

Reserves

Reserves are the sum of project’s reserve additions booked over the budget interval.

Unrisked Sales:  is a sum of working interest, gross reserves within the Budget Period.  The Unrisked Sales Reserves equation for n reserve records is given by:

∑ ((Gross Reserves within TB)n * WIn)

Risked Sales:  is a sum of the working interest, risked, gross reserve within the budget period.  The Risked Sales Reserves equation for n reserve records is given by:

∑ ((Gross Reserves within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)

 

Cumulative Production

The cumulative production is the sum of a project’s production over the budget interval.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, cumulative product over the budget period.  The Unrisked Sales Cumulative Production equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TB)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn))

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked, cumulative product over the budget period.  The Risked Sales Cumulative Production equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TB)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) * NPSn)

 

First Spend Cumulative Production

The first spend cumulative production is the sum of a project’s production for one year starting from the earliest project expenditure.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, cumulative product from the earliest expenditure date for one year. The Unrisked Sales First Spend Cumulative Prod equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TFS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn)),

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked cumulative product from the earliest expenditure date for one year.  The Risked Sales First Spend Cumulative Prod equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TFS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) * NPSn))

 

On-stream Cumulative Production

The Onstream Cumulative Production is the sum of a project’s production for one year starting from the earliest Onstream date.  

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, cumulative product from the earliest production Onstream date for one year. The Unrisked Sales Onstream Cumulative Prod equation for n production segments:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TOS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn))

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked, cumulative product from the earliest production on-stream date for one year.  The Risked Sales Onstream Cumulative Prod equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TOS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) * NPSn))

 

Annualized Production

Annualized Production is the average daily production rate over the Budget interval.  

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, average daily production rate over the budget period.  The Unrisked Sales Annualized Production equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TB)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) / ∆TB)

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked, average daily rate over the budget period.  The Risked Sales Annualized Production equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TB)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) * NPSn / ∆TB)

 

First Spend Annualized Production

First Spend Annualized Production is the average daily production rate over a one year period starting from the earliest project expenditure.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, cumulative product from the earliest expenditure date for one year.  The Unrisked Sales First Spend Annualized Prod equation for n production segments:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TFS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) / ∆TFS)

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked, cumulative product from the earliest expenditure date for one year.  The Risked Sales First Spend Annualized Prod equation for n production segments:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TFS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) * NPSn / ∆TFS)

 

On-stream Annualized Production

Onstream Annualized Production is the average daily rate over one year starting from the project’s earliest on-stream date.  

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, cumulative product from the earliest Onstream date for one year.  The Unrisked Sales Onstream Annualized Prod equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TOS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) / ∆TOS)

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked cumulative product from the earliest on-stream date for one year.  The Risked Sales Onstream Annualized Prod equation for n production segments is:

∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TOS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) * NPSn / ∆TOS)

 

Exit Rate

Exit Rate is the daily production rate on the last the day of the budget.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, production rate on the last day of the budget.  The Unrisked Sales Exit Rate equation for n production records is:

∑ (Gross Daily Production Rate at last day of TB) * WI * (1 - Shrink),

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked production rate on the last day of the budget.  The Risked Sales Exit Rate equation for n production records is:

∑ (Gross Daily Production Rate at last day of TB) * WI * (1 – Shrink) * NPS

 

First Spend Exit Rate

First Spend Exit Rate is the daily production rate one year after a project’s earliest expenditure.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, daily production one year from the earliest expenditure date.  The Unrisked Sales First Spend Exit Rate equation for n production records is:

∑ (Gross Daily Production Rate at last day of TFS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrink)n,

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked daily production one year from the earliest expenditure date.  The Risked Sales First Spend Exit Rate equation for n production records is:

∑ (Gross Daily Production Rate at last day of TFS)n * WIn * (1 – Shrink)n * NPSn

 

Onstream Exit Rate

Onstream Exit Rate is the daily production rate one year after a project’s earliest production on-stream date.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, daily production rate one year from the earliest production Onstream date.  The Unrisked Sales Onstream Exit Rate equation for n production records is:

∑ (Gross Daily Production Rate at last day of TFS)n * WIn * (1 - Shrink)n,

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked daily production rate one year from the earliest production Onstream date.  The Risked Sales Onstream Exit Rate equation for n production records is:

∑ (Gross Daily Production Rate at last day of TFS)n * WIn * (1 – Shrink)n * NPSn

 

F and D

F and D (F&D) is the sum of a project’s expenditures within the budget time frame divided by the sum of project’s reserve additions booked within the budget time frame.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross expenditures over the budget period, divided by working interest gross reserves over the budget period.  The Unrisked Sales F and D equation for n expenditure records and m reserve records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Reserves within TB)m * WIm)}

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, risked gross expenditures over the budget period, divided by working interest, risked gross reserves over the budget period:  The Risked Sales F and D equation for n expenditure records and m reserve records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Reserves within TB)m * WIm * NPSm)}

 

F and D Inverse - doesn't exist

F and D Inverse (F&D) is the sum of project’s reserve additions booked within the budget period divided by the sum of a project’s expenditures within the budget period.  Note that this metric avoids dividing by zero when no reserves are found.  

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross reserves over the budget period divided by working interest, gross expenditures over the budget period.  The Unrisked Sales F and D Inverse equation for n reserve records and m expenditure records is:

{∑ ((Gross Reserves within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)m * WIm)}

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, gross risked reserves over the budget period divided by working interest, risked, gross expenditures over the budget period.  The Risked Sales F and D Inverse equation for n reserve records and m expenditure records is:

{∑ ((Gross Reserves within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)m * WIm * NPSm)}

 

$ / Annualized Production

$ / Annualized ($/boe) is the sum of a project’s expenditures within the budget period divided by the average daily production rate over the Budget interval.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross expenditures over the budget period divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, average daily production rate over the budget period.  The Unrisked Sales F and D equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TB)n * WIn * (1 - Shrinkn) / ∆TB)}

Risked Sales:  is the sum of the working interest, risked, gross expenditures over the budget period divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked, average daily production rate over the budget period.  The Risked Sales $ / Annualized Production equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TB)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm) * NPSm) / ∆TB)}

 

$ / First Spend Annual Production

$ / First Spend Ann. Prod is the sum of a project’s expenditures within the budget interval divided by the average daily production rate over a one year period starting from the earliest project expenditure.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, average daily production rate for one year from the earliest expenditure date.  The Unrisked Sales $ / First Spend Ann. Prod equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TFS)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm)) / ∆TFS)}

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, risked gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked average daily production rate for one year from the earliest expenditure date.  The Risked Sales $ / First Spend Ann Prod equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TFS)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm) * NPSm) / ∆TFS)}

 

$ / Onstream Ann. Production

$ / Onstream Ann. Prod is the sum of a project’s expenditures over the budget interval divided by the average daily rate over one year starting from the project’s earliest on-stream date.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, and reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, average daily production rate for one year from the earliest on-stream date.  The Unrisked Sales $ / Onstream Ann. Prod equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TOS)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm)) / ∆TOS)}

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, risked gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked average daily production rate for one year from the first on-stream date  The Risked Sales $ / Onstream Ann. Prod equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production Over TOS)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm) * NPSm) / ∆TOS)}

 

$ / Exit Rate

$ / Exit Rate is the sum of a project’s expenditures within the budget interval divided by the daily production rate on the last the day of the budget.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, and reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, average daily production rate on the last day of the budget.  The Unrisked Sales $ / Exit Rate equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Daily Production at the last day of TB)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm)}

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, gross, risked expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked, average daily production rate on the last day of the budget.  The Risked Sales $ / Exit Rate equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Daily Production at the last day of TB)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm) * NPSm)}

 

$ / First Spend Exit Rate

$ / First Spend Exit Rate is the sum of a project’s expenditures within the budget interval divided by the daily production rate one year after a project’s earliest expenditure.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, and reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, average daily production rate one year from the earliest expenditure date.  The Unrisked Sales $ / First Spend Exit Rate equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Daily Production one year from first day of TFS)m * WIm* (1 – Shrinkm))}

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, gross, risked expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked, average daily production rate one year from the earliest expenditure date.  The Unrisked Sales $ / First Spend Exit Rate equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Daily Production one year from the first day of TFS)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm) * NPSm)}

 

$ / Onstream Exit Rate

$ / Onstream Exit Rate is the sum of a project’s expenditures within the budget interval divided by the daily production rate one year after a project’s earliest production on-stream date.

Unrisked Sales:  is the working interest, gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, and reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, average daily production rate one year from the earliest Onstream production date.  The Unrisked Sales $ / Onstream Exit Rate equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Daily Production one year from first day of TOS)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm))}

Risked Sales:  is the working interest, risked, gross expenditures over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked average daily production rate one year from the earliest Onstream production date.  The Risked Sales $ / Onstream Exit Rate equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((Gross Expenditures within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Daily Production one year from the first day of TOS)m * WIm * (1 – Shrinkm) * NPSm)}

 

Lifting Cost

Lifting cost is the sum of a project’s OPEX cost within the budget interval divided by the sum of a project’s production within the budget interval.

Unrisked Sales:  is the OPEX cost over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, cumulative production over the budget period.  The Unrisked Sales Lifting Cost equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((OPEX within TB)n * WIn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production over TB)m * WIm)}

Risked Sales:  is the risked OPEX cost over the Budget Period, divided by working interest, reduced by shrinkage, before royalty, risked cumulative production over the budget period.  The Unrisked Sales Lifting Cost equation for n expenditure records and m production records is:

{∑ ((OPEX within TB)n * WIn * NPSn)} / {∑ ((Gross Cumulative Production over TB)m * WIm * NPSm)}
