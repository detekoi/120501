Chart Types

The following outlines the available Chart choices from the "Chart Type" list. Click on a Chart Type, then click on a subtype to select a new type. For most graphs, the default is a Bar Chart.

Bar

  • Side-by-Side: shows bars from each series one beside the other. These bars are arranged such that they are the same width. The width of the bars will depend on the number of series being displayed.
  • Stacked: shows bars stacked one atop the other. The positive and negative values are stacked separately above and below the origin.
  • Percent Stacked:show bars stacked one on top of the other in such a way that the total height of the stacked bar (from its lowest to highest points) is 100.

Line

  • Overlay: shows lines from each series independent of one another. The lines are shown joining the values from a single series.
  • Stacked: shows lines stacked one atop the other. The positive and negative values are stacked separately above and below the origin.
  • Percent Stacked:show lines stacked one on top of the other in such a way that the total height of the stacked bar (from its lowest to highest points) is 100.

Area

  • Overlay: shows areas from each series independent of one another. The areas are shown joining the values from a single series.
  • Stacked: shows areas stacked one atop the other. The positive and negative values are stacked separately above and below the origin.
  • Percent Stacked:show areas stacked one on top of the other in such a way that the total height of the stacked bar (from its lowest to highest points) is 100.

Pie: shows values as slices of a pie. The size of each slice is proportional to its value in relation to the whole. Pie charts for multiple series are plotted as multiple pies, one for each series.

Meter

  • Standard: shows a single dial with region(s) behind it.
  • Super-imposed: shows multiple dials all with shared region(s). The dials overlap so that it can represent multiple needles in a single region.

Scatter: show the values arranged on the plot using the category and value data as co-ordinates. Each data value is indicated by a marker.

Stock:

  • Candle-stick: contains a box with lines extending out of the top and bottom. The upper and lower edges of the box show the open and close values. The upper and lower points of the line show the high and low values.
  • Bar-stick: shows a vertical line with two horizontal lines sticking to it. The upper and lower points of the vertical line show the stock open and close values. The two horizontal lines are the high and low values.

Bubble: show the values arranged on the plot using the category and value data as co-ordinates. Each data value is indicated by a marker.

Difference: use two fills to represent positive and negative areas.

Gantt: contains a connection line between a start and end marker.

Tube

  • Side-by-Side: shows tubes from each series one beside the other. These tubes are arranged such that they are the same width. The width of the bars will depend on the number of series being displayed.
  • Stacked: shows tubes stacked one atop the other. The positive and negative values are stacked separately above and below the origin.
  • Percent Stacked:show tubes stacked one on top of the other in such a way that the total height of the stacked bar (from its lowest to highest points) is 100.

Cone

  • Side-by-Side: shows tubes from each series one beside the other. These tubes are arranged such that they are the same width. The width of the bars will depend on the number of series being displayed.
  • Stacked: shows tubes stacked one atop the other. The positive and negative values are stacked separately above and below the origin.
  • Percent Stacked:show tubes stacked one on top of the other in such a way that the total height of the stacked bar (from its lowest to highest points) is 100.

Pyramid

  • Side-by-Side: shows tubes from each series one beside the other. These tubes are arranged such that they are the same width. The width of the bars will depend on the number of series being displayed.
  • Stacked: shows tubes stacked one atop the other. The positive and negative values are stacked separately above and below the origin.
  • Percent Stacked:show tubes stacked one on top of the other in such a way that the total height of the stacked bar (from its lowest to highest points) is 100.
