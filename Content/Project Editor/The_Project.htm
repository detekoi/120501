The Project

The Project is how esi.manage encapsulates an ActivityExpenditures and the corresponding OutcomeProduction, Reserves Adds, Operating Expenses. The AssetWell, Facility, Cost Center is how the various MeasuresExpenditures, Production, Reserves, Operating Expenses are tied together within the Project.

During the Budgeting process, this encapsulation allows Projects to be compared - Performance or Economic Metrics can be calculated for the Projects of interest and the relative performance evaluated.

In addition, a Project also contains a Hierarchy - business process information (Geographic Region, Asset Team) that allows a Project to be reported on appropriately.

There is a Life Cycle to a Project, represented by the combination of the Project Status as well as the types of data that is contained within the Project. The following list indicates how a Project can progress - it is not required that a Project moves through every phase listed below.

  1. Long Range Plan - an Activity that is possible/planned for some time in the future.
  2. Planned - a Project that is part of the current year's Budget. The Project data type is Forecast - what is expected to happen in terms of Expenditures and Production, Reserves and Operating Expenses.
  3. In Progress - once a Project's activities begin, the Progress can be tracked by entering Actuals (Field Estimates, Actuals, etc.) against the various measures.
  4. Complete - when all of the activities are completed, and the Production is On-Stream, Production and OPEX Actuals can continue to be tracked. The Asset (well) is now part of the Base Production in future years.

esi.manage provides the ability to apply Risk against the Forecast numbers. For Expenditures, Risk is represented by the Probability of Doing - the likelihood that the Expenditure is going to occur. For Production, Reserves and Operating Expenses, Risk is represented by the Probability of Success - the likelihood of realizing the Outcome. Typically, the Probability of Success is based on historical information - i.e., in a certain region new wells are successful 85% of the time.

Within the Project itself, Risk is entered on the Dependencies page. It is beyond the Project boundaries where the impact of Risk becomes evident:

  • Fiscal Targets - Reporting or Ranking using a Risked basis reflects the likely outcome at the end of the Year for a group of Projects. Most will be successful, some won't. The Risked Forecast values represent the historical success.
  • Comparing Projects - in most cases, Projects should be compared on both an Unrisked as well as Risked basis. While a Project may have a higher upside, it may also come with a higher degree of Risk.
