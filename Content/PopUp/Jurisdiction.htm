Jurisdiction

Jurisdictions model the burdens on project economics put in place by the regimes governing the regions production is coming from. Jurisdictional economic burdens can be used to model taxes, special participation expenses, government tariffs, and other factors external to the project that effect its financial feasibility.

Two levels of Jurisdictions can be configured:

  1. Government - represents the 'level' where Expenditure Depreciation can be applied.
  2. Local - represents the level of government where the Asset exists.

A Local jurisdiction belongs to a Government Jurisdiction. Each Project is assigned a Government Level  jurisdiction on the project details screen when the project is first created. The individual Assets can be assigned a Local Jurisdiction independently, on the Burdens page of the Project.

Additional modifiers can be added against either the production or revenue within a given jurisdiction by adding Tax Modifiers.
