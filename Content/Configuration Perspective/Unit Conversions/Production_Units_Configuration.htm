Production Units Configuration

Defining the Production Units and Conversions involves multiple Configuration screens. The overall process is described in Production Units Overview.

One aspect of the configuration is defining the Production Units. For each unit you will need to provide one conversion factor. Note that the (energy) and (mass) labels are provided to indicate what the underlying Quality basis is (Gas is energy, NonGas is mass):

  • For Gas Volume units - the conversion is to m3 (energy).
  • For Gas Energy units - the conversion is to J (energy).
  • For NonGas Volume units - the conversion is to m3 (mass).
  • for NonGas Mass units - the conversion is to kg (mass).
  • For Equivalence units - the conversion is to boe.

The conversions provided here, combined with Production Quality and reference Product conversions enable interconversion between volume and quality for a product, as well as between products for equivalence reporting.

 

The fundamental units in esi.manage are:

  • Gas
  • cubic metres (m3) for volume
  • joules (J) for energy
  • Non-Gas
  • cubic metres (m3) for volume
  • kilograms (kg) for mass
  • Equivalence
  • Barrels of oil equivalent (boe)

 

In esi.manage, Gas is referred to as “energy”.

 

For instance, the Products configuration table has an Energy column that is used to specify whether the product is Gas or Non-Gas. Equivalence production units are used when reporting both Gas and Non-Gas products in one number. The typical equivalence production unit is boe or mboe but  the user can define other equivalence production units as required.

Every production unit entered in the Production Units table must have a conversion factor to exactly one of the fundamental units. By and large these conversion factors are basic physical facts so finding the right conversion factor is merely a matter of looking it up in an appropriate reference. (An exception is cf to m3 conversion. It should be 3.283 (0.0283168)

Reading the Production Units table is intuitive. The conversion is always from one user-defined production unit to X fundamental units. E.g. 1 cf = 2.817399E-2 m3 (energy).

 

Table Options

Note that a Unit can only have one conversion entered, it is either a Volumetric unit (converts to m3), Quality (converts to J or kg), or Equivalence (converts to boe).

The column that the conversion is entered in defines what type of Unit it is (Volumetric or Quality), and how this unit will be utilized within the conversion infrastructure. The m3 (energy) and m3 (mass) headers indicate which column to enter the Volumetric conversion factor into: a Gas unit is entered under m3 (energy) and NonGas unit under m3 (mass).

With the exception of Equivalence units, the conversion factors entered on this page should be standard conversions. When providing Volumetric conversions, they should all use the same Reference state, i.e.; mcf and mmcf should be factors of 1000 and 1000000 to the cf conversion.

Equivalence units will be based on your company's practice, however, similar units (mcfe and mmcfe or mboe and mmboe) should be numerically consistent.

 

To Add A Production Unit

  1. Scroll to the last line in the table (it will be empty).
  2. Enter the Abbreviation (up to 8 characters). This is what will appear in Drop Down lists.
  3. Enter the Description (up to 50 characters).
  4. Enter the appropriate conversion:
    • mcf is a Gas Volume unit (m3 (energy)).
    • The conversion factor is From mcf To m3.
    • btu is a Gas Energy unit (J (energy)).
    • The conversion factor is From btu To J.
  5. Click outside the line to save the change.

 

Changing an Existing Unit

You can edit any entry in an existing row. Note that changes to a conversion factor will not propagate into existing data that is associated with that unit.

Deleting a Production Unit that is in use is not allowed.
