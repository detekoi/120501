Price Model

The calculation of Revenue in esi.manage is comprised of three systems:

  • Production Calculations for a particular Asset
  • Product Quality (when Prices are expressed in quality dependant units, like $/mmbtu)
  • Price Profile

Other topics describe the Production Calculation and Product Quality components of esi.manage.

 

Terminology

  • Effective Price – the particular product price that is calculated for a particular Price Forecast.
  • Price Forecast – made up of root forecasts and price adjustment forecasts.  The root price is generally the base price for a specific product (i.e. NYMEX for Gas, WTI for Oil) and is entered as a single number.  A price adjustment is nested off of a root price (or off of another price adjustment)
  • Price Adjustment – a two part entry (Scalar and Offset) that represents the desired adjustment at a particular date.
  • Price Details – a time series of Price Adjustments for a particular Price Forecast.
  • Scenario - a way of declaring different values for your existing forecasts. (i.e. high price, low price, archived prices)
  • Profiles – price forecasts can be combined into profiles based on % of production, or based on volume amounts, which will then give an effective price based on the different forecasts provided.
  • Tier - ties a forecast to a specific profile and specifies the allocation of that forecast.  Tiers are allocated and resolved sequentially.

 

Effective Price

The "Effective Price" is what will be used to calculate the Revenue for a particular product. In the following screen shot, the highlight is on a particular Price Forecast (Rockies - Gas), at a particular period of time (January 2010).

The bottom of the screen shows how the "Price" (USD/mmbtu) will be determined:

  1. NYMX Gas is the root Price Forecast.
  2. For this particular Scenario (Current Prices), NYMX Gas has a Price of $5.02 USD/mmbtu.
  3. Rockies Gas is a Price Forecast which has been related to NYMX Gas.
  4. It has a Scale of 1, and an offset of -0.25.
  5. The Effective Price for the Rockies Gas Forecast is 4.77 USD/mmbtu.
  6. A Price Adjustment is made up of two parts:
  7. Scale - which is a fractional multiplier.
  8. Offset - which is a numeric offset applied after the multiplier.

A Global Forecast, such as "AB Par Price Oil", defines the currency unit and basis (mcf, bbl, mmbtu) for all Price Forecasts which are derived from it.

Global Forecasts don't have a Scale, only a Price.

 

The Pricing Model is configured through four nodes within the Configuration:

 

  • Scenarios are a "named" set of Prices, and Scale and Offset Modifier.
  • All Forecasts are accessible to each Scenario. However, the Forecast Details can be modified for each Scenario to enable Price Sensitivity analysis
  • Defaults allow specific Price Profiles to be assigned to each Product. This will initialize each Product with a Price Forecast.
  • Forecasts are where the hierarchical relationships between Price Forecasts are defined.
  • Profiles are where the functional (contract) relationships between Price Forecasts are defined.
