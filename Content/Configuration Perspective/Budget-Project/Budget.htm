Budgets

This section describes how to add, edit, and modify the details of the budgets in esi.manageTM . Access to this screen is restricted to users with Configuration Perspective access permissions.

The type of current budget you are working with will have a considerable impact on what you can do with it. There are three classifications to "budgets".

  • Budget: A budget is an active, ongoing budget that represents the resources allocated to a given project and the capital expected to be returned on it. A budget is essentially a "work in progress", holding those projects which are just going online, or have not yet produced a large return. In theory, budgets would exist for each individual fiscal year, perhaps broken down by region *if* that is the organization of the corporation.
  • Forecast: A Forecast is created when a Budget is closed. Closing a budget occurs when the planning phase has passed for this budget, you cannot go back in time and adjust how much money was predicted to be spent on and the production returned from a given project, however the continued returns and ongoing costs may fluctuate from the original numbers. Once a Budget moves to Forecast status, it becomes the primary information repository now that the original budget cannot be changed or added to. New projects may be added and old ones that are no longer viable can be removed. "Actuals" and "Field Estimates" are now available for editing, and AFE's can now be assigned and tracked. 
  • Archive: You can Archive an open forecast by selecting one of the Archive options. This is usually done on a monthly or quarterly basis, when the company wants to create a snapshot of their current position compared to where they were. Archives provide lookbacks and comparisons, a benchmark for further planning to be based against. Reports can still be generated out of an archive, but depending on whether it is Closed or Editable, you may or may not be able to add more data to the Projects contained in the Budget.
  • Closed and Editable Archives: You may wish to leave your archive open to revisions or not. Leaving it editable means you can correct errors or update inaccurate information, but leaving it closed allows you to ensure nobody deliberately tampers with the data. You have a choice which type of archive to create by selecting the "Closed Archive Budget" or "Editable Archive Budget" buttons.

Having a saved copy of the original budget allows companies to compare differences or variances between the original plan and the current forecast.

In the esi.manage data hierarchy, a budget contains a set of projects.

 

 

Budgets can be set to be:

  • (In)visible in the rest of the application
  • Read only

With so many uses, budgets are known by different names:

  • Budget type "Budget"
  • Necessary precursor to a Forecast or Scenario
  • Should not be used to hold projects
  • Budget Type "Current Forecast"
  •  Created from a Budget
  •  Evergreen collection of base and inventory projects
  • Budget Type "Archive"
  • Created from a Forecast
  • Can be Closed or Editable
  • Template Budget
    • A forecast specifically used in esi.manage to hold template (also known as type) projects
  • Plan
    • Another name for a Forecast
  • Scenario
    • A budget that is used as a "sandbox" to evaluate different forecast scenarios.

Note: Your company may use terminology that differs from this taxonomy. For instance, many companies refer to a Current Forecast as a Budget.
