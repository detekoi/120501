Group Economic Limit

Overview

Previously, the economic limit calculations in esi.manage occurred at the well. Based on the Truncation Method for the Analysis Case, each asset had its economic limit determined. Cost only assets were not truncated. From a reporting perspective, the individual Hierarchy nodes in the Fiscal model collected the truncated cash flows for all of the assets below that node, and would calculate an economic limit for the node based on the aggregated cash flows. This process continued up the Hierarchy tree.

The Group Economic Limit calculation option available in esi.manage introduces a new Group behaviour. Based on the selected Hierarchy node(s) – which can be different than the Hierarchy used for the Fiscal Model, the economic limit that is calculated at a Group node is then “propagated” through the child assets of the node such that the resultant cash flow for any asset (and by association, the node itself) will be the minimum of the Group, and the individual asset.

When multiple Hierarchy nodes are involved in the particular analysis, it is the truncated cash flow that is propagated up to the next level. The end result is that, for a particular asset, its economic limit (and cashflows) will be the minimum of its economic limit date and the minimum date for any Group Limit nodes above it.

Note that: by enabling Group Economic Limit in the Configuration, this becomes a Group Concern, the same as Curtailment and Price Tiers. Regardless of how many projects are selected from a particular budget (and the selected Analysis Case has Truncation being calculated), all of the Projects that are within the Group Hierarchy tree will be solved. Only the selected projects will be reported, but their results will be based on the calculation methodology described above.

Cost Only assets still require the Cost Only flag to be turned on, otherwise they will be truncated immediately.

Configuration of Group Economic Limit

Group Economic Limit is “configured” within the Fiscal Modelling node in the esi.manage Configuration.

All of the available Hierarchy trees can be selected for performing the Group Calculations.

If there are node(s) within the Tree that you don’t want the Group behaviour applied to, you can add these by selecting the green + symbol:

With this configuration, assets that fall within the selected Hierarchy will be calculated as normal, however, the cash flow that is propagated up to nodes above (Area 6) will not be truncated at the Group Limit, and the Economic Limit date calculated at hierarchy nodes further up the tree will not be “propagated” down through projects under the exclusion node.

In most situations, a single Hierarchy will be selected. The option exists to use multiple Hierarchies for the Economic Limit Grouping, however, you must ensure that the calculation paths between the two Hierarchies do not overlap. This will require the use of the exclusion option to exclude nodes such that any Project (and its asset) will only be dealt with under one path.
