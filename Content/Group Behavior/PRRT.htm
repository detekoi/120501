PRRT (Petroleum Resource Rent Tax)

The Petroleum Resource Rent Tax (PRRT) is a profit-based tax which is levied on Australian petroleum projects.

PRRT is currently applied to the recovery of all petroleum products from Australian Government waters (including crude oil, natural gas, liquid petroleum gas (LPG) condensate and ethane), except for petroleum products extracted from the North West Shelf project and the Joint Petroleum Development Area, and value added products such as liquefied natural gas (LNG).

esi.manage supports the PRRT tax calculation through the use of an economic concession group that is specific to Australia. The PRRT concession group allows you to specify a group of projects and set a number of Expenditure Cost pool rules with associated tax rates.  As part of the economics calculations, PRRT calculation is ring-fenced for the projects that are a part of the group.
