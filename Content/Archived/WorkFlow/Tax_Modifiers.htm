Tax Modifiers

  1. Tax Modifiers are additional criteria that you can use to define additional Production or Revenue impacts within a jurisdiction.
  2. These can either be Production,Revenue or Fixed Taxes.

 

Production Tax

 

  1. A Production Tax is configured as a Currency / Volume tax.
  2. What will be entered into the Jurisdiction Table will be Amounts (corresponding to the specified Currency).
  3. The calculated tax will be the Production (in the specified volume unit) multiplied by the Amount for the corresponding time period.
  4. As such, a Production Tax is not affected by the Price File being used for calculations.

 

Revenue Tax

 

  1. A Revenue Tax is configured as a Percentage of Production tax.
  2. What will be entered into the Jurisdiction Table will be Royalty %.
  3. The calculated tax will be the specified % of the calculated Revenue for the corresponding time period.
  4. As such, a Revenue Tax is affected by the Price File being used for calculations.

 

Net Profit Royalty

 

  1. A Net Production Revenue Tax is applied to revenues, minus the allowable depreciated expenditures.
  2. What will be entered into the Jurisdiction Table will be Royalty %.
  3. The calculated tax will be the specified % of the calculated Revenue for the corresponding time period.
  4. The NPR will only be applied in years in which there is a calculated profit.

 

Fixed Tax

  1. A Fixed Tax is independent of any cash flow.
  2. It is computed for every month within the calculation time window, whether other cash flows are present or not.
  3. It is input as a fixed tax amount for a specific month and the specified tax amounts will be applied for every month that lies within the calculation time window.
